Introduction

Instagram is an American photo and video sharing social networking service owned by Meta Platforms. It allows users to upload media that can be edited with filters, be organized by hashtags, and be associated with a location via geographical tagging. Posts can be shared publicly or with preapproved followers.

Strong design choices

  • Instagram's predominantly white background with blue accents and muted colors is visually pleasing and non-intrusive. This design choice puts the focus on user-generated content and images, providing a clean and modern aesthetic.
  • The placement of primary icons (home feed, search, plus sign, heart, and user profile) at the bottom of the screen in the mobile app adheres to ergonomic design principles. These icons are easily reachable with one-handed use and can be accessed with just one touch.
  • The use of consistent and intuitive icons aids in user recognition and engagement. Icons like the heart for likes and the magnifying glass for search are universally recognizable.
  • The use of swipe gestures, such as swiping left or right to navigate between posts, is a user-friendly design choice that aligns with modern mobile interaction patterns.
  • Users can customize their Instagram experience by following accounts, and saving content. The "Not Interested" and "Interested" options help refine content recommendations.

Weak design choices

  • While necessary for monetization, the integration of ads into the feed can disrupt the overall design and user experience, often feeling incongruent with the surrounding content.
  • Instagram's design lacks options for users to personalize their profile or the app's overall look, which could provide a more tailored experience for individuals.

Target audience

According to Hootsuite (2022), most users of Instagram are Millenial and Gen Z users, and global internet users aged 16-24 prefer Instagram to other social media sites, making it a Gen Z favorite. The age of Instagram users are broken down below:
  • 13-17 years old: 8.5%
  • 18-24 years old: 30.1%
  • 25-34 years old: 31.5%
  • 35-44 years old: 16.1%
  • 45-54 years old: 8%
  • 55-64 years old: 3.6%
  • 65 years old and up: 2.1%
However, only 15% of teens reported that Instagram is their most used application in 2018. Both YouTube and Snapchat received double this percentage, with the former receiving 32% and the latter receiving 35%. This suggests that Instagram is continuing to grow its customer base and popularity.
In terms of a gender demographic, this is almost evenly split with 50.7% of users being male and 49.3% being female in general, but for users over the age of 35, women outnumber men. Non-binary users were not considered in this survey, although there are such users on the app.
As for location demographics, the biggest percentage of Instagram users compared to other countries and territories are in India, U.S., Brazil, Indonesia, and Russia. India, however, has TikTok (another renowned and popular social media site) banned, which may be the reason Instagram has so many downloads – citizens have to resort to the next most similar substitute platform.
