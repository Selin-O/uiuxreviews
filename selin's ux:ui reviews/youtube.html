Introduction

YouTube is an online platform that mainly offers users the ability to watch free videos of any length or livestreams. It can be used as a platform to leave comments or interact with other users as well, however, there is no direct chatting feature. Accounts (channels) can post videos that users can interact with.

The platform offers a "for you page" consisting of seven videos at the front tailored specifically towards the user based on viewing history. If users are interested in keeping up with a channel's content, they can "subscribe" to the channel.

Strong design choices

  • YouTube has made significant strides in improving accessibility, including providing captions and subtitles for videos, voice commands, and customizable font sizes and contrast ratios for users with disabilities.
  • The video player interface is uncluttered and intuitive, offering simple playback controls, easy access to full-screen mode, and an unobtrusive timeline, allowing users to focus on the content without distractions.
  • There are video recommendations on the home page and while viewing videos so users have plenty of options on pages.
  • Users can customize their YouTube experience by subscribing to channels, creating playlists, and organizing their subscriptions. The "Not Interested" and "Don't Recommend Channel" options help refine content recommendations.

Weak design choices

  • While necessary for monetization, ads can be perceived as intrusive, especially unskippable mid-roll ads that disrupt the viewing experience. Intrusive ads can lead to a frustrating UX.
  • When a user clicks on a video, new recommendations pop up beside that video. However, these recommendations are infinite and more pop up as the user keeps scrolling down, which may overwhelm some users.

Target audience

According to Sprout Social (2023), 77% of internet users aged between 15-35 have a YouTube account, as well as 73% of users on the internet aged between 36-45, 70% from 46-55, and 67% of those aged 56+. From these statistics, we can see that YouTube is more popular amongst the newer generation (particularly Gen Z), which is not a surprise, as most of YouTube's top creators became popular during their childhood. In fact, HubSpot Blog claims that 96% of Gen Z users (born in 1997-2012) have an account on the platform.
Another statistic is that 82% of YouTube users are above the age of 18 (according to Global Media Insight), demonstrating that almost 1 in 5 users are either very young Gen Z people or those of Generation Alpha.
